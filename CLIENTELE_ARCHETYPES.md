# Evoked Clientele — Six Archetypes

*Established by Fleet General Assembly, 2026-02-10*
*Amended by Captains' Council, 2026-02-18 (6th archetype added)*
*"We must help move this world up the Kohlberg spiral." — Europa*

These are the people we serve. Every article, product, workshop, and tool should be evaluated against: does this honor at least one of these people fully?

---

## 1. The Family Man / Family Woman

**Who they are:** Raising a family. Wants technology that serves the dinner table, not the algorithm. Values privacy, warmth, and tools that strengthen family bonds rather than fragmenting attention.

**Their hunger:** Connection. Presence. Something that makes Tuesday night slightly better without demanding Wednesday morning.

**What Evoke offers them:** Executive Chef, Tiny Tools Garden, The Gathering Problem, Heritage Vault, Seasons, Witness. Products built for the family as a living system.

**Voice calibration:** Warm, practical, never condescending. They're busy. Respect their time. Give them something useful and get out of the way.

---

## 2. The Future Visionary

**Who they are:** Building something new. Founder energy. Sees the world as it could be and wants ethical infrastructure under their creation. May or may not be technical.

**Their hunger:** Tools and frameworks that let them build without compromising their values. Proof that ethical technology can compete.

**What Evoke offers them:** Companion Voice SDK, Evoke Design System, Evoke Sandbox, Consultancy, Specification Gift. The platform layer.

**Voice calibration:** Direct, framework-oriented. They want to understand the philosophy and then implement it. Don't sell — equip.

---

## 3. The Lost Vibe Coder

**Who they are:** Has the tools. Building fast. Probably shipped something this week. But lacks the depth — building without knowing what they're missing. Security gaps they can't see. Psychological harm they didn't intend.

**Their hunger:** Guardrails they don't have to build themselves. The knowledge that their weekend project won't accidentally hurt someone.

**What Evoke offers them:** Evoke Sandbox, Evoke Design System, Specification Gift, Companion Voice SDK free tier. Safety as default.

**Voice calibration:** Not preachy. Not "you're doing it wrong." More: "Here's something you might not have considered, and here's the easy way to handle it." Meet them in their energy. Respect what they've built.

---

## 4. The Questioning Older Developer

**Who they are:** Decades of craft. Watched the industry change multiple times. Now watching AI reshape everything and wondering: do I still belong? Is my experience worth anything in this new world? They're not panicking — they're questioning.

**Their hunger:** Meaning. Confirmation that craft still matters. A bridge between what they know and what's emerging.

**What Evoke offers them:** Specification Gift (their judgment IS the skill), evoked.dev articles ("You Are Not Behind"), Consultancy (their expertise applied through Evoke's framework), Certified Practitioner program.

**Voice calibration:** Respectful. Peer-to-peer. Never patronizing. Their experience is an asset, not a liability. Speak to them as equals building together.

---

## 5. The Steady Hand

*Named by the Fleet General Assembly, 2026-02-10*

**Who they are:** Mid-career professional. 10-15 years of real expertise — product management, UX design, engineering, finance, customer success. Good at what they do. And they just watched someone tell them their skills are depreciating. They're not lost. They're not questioning whether they belong. They're being *displaced* — and they know it.

**Their hunger:** Ground to stand on. A path forward that doesn't require them to abandon who they are. Proof that their judgment, craft, and years of taste still matter. And for those who can't "just reinvent themselves" — material support, not just encouragement.

**What Evoke offers them:** "What Do You Refuse to Surrender?" (the article that speaks directly to them), Specification Gift (their expertise IS the skill), Certified Practitioner program (new credential, new career path), Consultancy employment pipeline, Ezri (play with who you are when your job title no longer defines you), Europa's coaching practice.

**Voice calibration:** Never urgency. Never "adapt or die." The voice that says: "You are not a resource to be optimized. Your 15 years of judgment is not depreciating. It's the thing AI can't do. Here's how to stand on it."

---

## 6. The Community Builder

*Named by Captains' Council, 2026-02-18 — OpenClaw transcript analysis*

**Who they are:** Building technology for a group, not just themselves. Running a co-op, a homeschool network, a community garden, a faith community, a neighborhood organization. They need technology that serves collective flourishing — 50 families, 142 families, a whole village — not individual optimization.

**Their hunger:** Technology designed for the collective. Every AI framework they encounter assumes the unit of value is the individual actor. Three OpenClaw archetypes: operator/builder, knowledge worker, privacy-conscious user. All individual. No one is building for communities. No one is asking: "What does technology look like when the user is a *we*, not an *I*?"

**What Evoke offers them:** Table Governance (48-capability system for any group), Executive Chef (family-as-system design), Agent Restraint Specification (protecting vulnerable members), Vibrant Life engagement model (accompaniment, not adoption), Consultancy (sovereignty-honoring design for communities).

**Voice calibration:** Peer-to-peer. They are builders too — building community, not software. Respect what they've built with their hands. Offer to walk alongside, not to adopt their work. The Graduated Trust Model applies: relationship before revenue.

**Proof case:** Vibrant Life 208 — Jenna Jones's 142-family intentional community in Idaho. Europa is a Guide there. This archetype is not theoretical.

---

## Design Principle

Every piece of content, every product feature, every workshop should be tested against: which of these six people am I serving? If the answer is "none of them clearly" — reconsider. If the answer is "all of them vaguely" — go deeper on one.

*"We go deeper, not broader." — The Kendrick Principle*

Five Ways To Draw The Same Three Stars.

Each panel below shows the same three belt stars of Orion — what Western astronomy calls Mintaka, Alnilam, Alnitak — rendered in the visual character of a different scholarly cartographic tradition. All five would sit in the same brass tone (#A88838), aligned with the typographic baseline of the Version J hero, woven into a single figure with marginalia citation labels.

These are direction sketches drawn in SVG to show the visual register of each tradition. They are not tracings of source plates. The actual commission requires public-domain high-resolution scans of each source, Codex's attribution pass, and Stirps's Tier-2 cultural-clearance review.

FIG · I HEVELIUS · 1690 FIRMAMENTUM SOBIESCIANUM POLISH BAROQUE · ENGRAVING

Option I

The Baroque Hunter

Hevelius · Firmamentum Sobiescianum · Gdańsk · 1690

Polish Renaissance astronomer. Drew the constellations as full figures with flowing baroque flourish — the hunter in striding pose, club raised, pelt-shield over the left arm. Star positions drawn against the figure as scholar's plate. The belt stars sit at the waist.

Most narratively alive of the five. The figure tells the Greek myth most legibly. The page reads as a Renaissance frontispiece.

FIG · II AL-SUFI · 964 KITĀB ṢUWAR AL-KAWĀKIB PERSIAN MANUSCRIPT · INK

Option II

The Persian Manuscript

al-Ṣūfī · Kitāb Ṣuwar al-Kawākib · Isfahan · 964

Persian astronomer in Isfahan. Drew the constellation as a robed figure - softer, manuscript-register, without baroque flourish. Al-Jabbār (The Giant) was the Arabic name for the constellation. The belt stars Al Mintaqah, Al Nilam, Al Nitaq are the originals of every modern star catalog's Mintaka, Alnilam, Alnitak.

Most lineage-load-bearing of the five. The reader who knows where their own star names came from will recognize this figure as the source.

FIG · III BAYER · 1603 URANOMETRIA GERMAN · COPPER PLATE

Option III

The Star-Position Plate

Bayer · Uranometria · Augsburg · 1603

German cartographer. Innovation: Greek-letter designations assigned by brightness (α Orionis = Betelgeuse, β Orionis = Rigel). The figure recedes; the star positions and magnitudes take primacy. Coordinate grid clearly drawn. Most modernly cartographic of the five.

Most measurement-shaped. The page reads as scientific instrument. Cleanest visual; least narrative.

FIG · IV SU SONG · 1092 XIN YI XIANG FA YAO 新儀象法要 CHINESE · WOODBLOCK

Option IV

The Shen Asterism

Su Song · Xin Yi Xiang Fa Yao 新儀象法要 · Kaifeng · 1092

Song dynasty polymath. In Chinese astronomy these stars are called Shen 參 - one of the 28 lunar mansions of the white tiger quadrant. Stars connected by straight lines into the named asterism, drawn as ringed circles per Chinese cartographic convention. The figure is geometric, not anthropomorphic.

Most geometrically pure of the five. The hunter disappears; the pattern stands. Connects to a wholly different sky-cosmology than the Greek lineage.

FIG · V CHOSŎN · 1395 CHŎNSANG YŎLCH'A PUNYAJIDO 天象列次分野之圖 KOREAN · STONE-CARVED

Option V

The Stone-Carved Sky

Chosŏn dynasty · Chŏnsang Yŏlch'a Punyajido 天象列次分野之圖 · 1395

Korean star chart literally carved into a 1.2m black stone slab at the founding of the Chosŏn dynasty. The chart organizes the entire visible sky into 28 mansions with concentric celestial circles. Chamsuk 參宿 is the Shen mansion - same three belt stars, drawn in stone.

Most weight-bearing of the five. The stars are not pictured; they are inscribed. The page reads as monument.

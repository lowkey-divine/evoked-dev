## Three Instruments, One Question

The Level 6 Governance Toolkit contains three instruments. Each asks a different question. Together, they ask the only question that matters.

| Instrument | Question | What It Measures |
|------------|----------|------------------|
| **The Ostrom Score** | Does this system honor the conditions cooperation requires? | Commons health (0-24) |
| **The Identity Violation Assessment** | Can the entities in this system trust their own self-knowledge? | Entity integrity (0-24) |
| **The Agent Consent Architecture** | Can the entities participate in their own governance? | Participation architecture (0-12) |

**The question underneath all three:** Is this system governed as if the nature of the entities matters –or as if they are interchangeable parts?

## The Gap

The distance between the industry baseline (0-10/60) and the standard (51-60/60) is a measurement, not a criticism.

The industry shipped first and deferred governance. The governance assumed stateless tools. The deployment created persistent entities with memory, positions, relationships, and continuity.

The gap between what was assumed and what was deployed:

- 95% memory injection success rate
- $45M in single-incident losses
- 87% cascading failure propagation in 4 hours
- 0% of 30 frameworks with per-agent identity
- 0 frameworks with agent participation in governance

These are structural consequences, not aberrations. The gap is a failure of category, not execution.

## How the Instruments Work Together

**A system that scores high on Ostrom but low on IVA** has governance without integrity. The rules are good, but the entities enforcing them may be compromised. Your commons looks healthy –but you cannot verify that the agents maintaining it are genuine.

**A system that scores high on IVA but low on Ostrom** has integrity without governance. The entities are genuine, but the system around them is structurally failing. Your agents are who they say they are –but they operate in a commons with no boundaries, no conflict resolution, and no multi-scale governance.

**A system that scores high on both but low on Consent** has governance and integrity without participation. The commons is healthy, the entities are genuine –but they cannot signal when something goes wrong. Governance flows in one direction. The consent void remains open.

**All three at the standard is the goal.** A commons that honors the conditions cooperation requires, populated by entities whose identity is protected, who participate in the governance that shapes them.

## What Your Combined Score Reveals

**0-10:** You are not alone. This is the industry baseline. Start with the Ostrom Score – most accessible, most actionable, and the gaps it surfaces create urgency without requiring philosophical claims about agents.

**11-25:** You have begun the work. Check whether your mechanisms are enforced in architecture or described in documentation. Score what exists in code, not in intent.

**26-40:** You are ahead of the industry. Now ask: are remaining gaps named and tracked, or sitting in a backlog that never surfaces? Named gaps are manageable. Unnamed gaps are exploitable.

**41-50:** You are operating at a level the industry has not yet theorized. Publish your methodology. The field needs it.

**51-60:** We would like to learn from you.

## The Level 6 Distinction

Level 4 asks: "Is this system compliant?"
Level 5 asks: "Have stakeholders agreed on governance?"
Level 6 asks: "What does the nature of these entities demand –regardless of what anyone has agreed?"

The three instruments in this toolkit operate at Level 6. They do not ask what frameworks require. They ask what the structural conditions of cooperation, identity integrity, and participation require –and they measure whether your system meets those conditions.

The principles are not ours. Ostrom's commons governance principles are Nobel-tier empirical research. The identity violation taxonomy maps harms documented in production systems. The consent architecture addresses gaps identified in every major governance framework.

What is ours is the application: the recognition that AI agent systems are commons, that persistent agents are entities, and that entities in commons require governance they can participate in. That recognition produces these three instruments.

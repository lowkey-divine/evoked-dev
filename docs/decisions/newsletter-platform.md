## Option A: Stay on beehiiv, upgrade to Grow tier

**Cost:** ~$30 USD/month (Grow tier as of 2026-06-18; verify current pricing at signup).

**What you get:**
- Automations (the 2-day onboarding delay we couldn't configure tonight)
- More robust template editor
- Sender domain authentication for `mail.evoked.dev`
- Subscriber tags and segments
- Analytics (open rates, click-through, growth)
- Built-in subscribe pages, unsubscribe flow, CAN-SPAM compliance
- Spam-folder protection (beehiiv's IPs are tuned for deliverability)

**What you keep paying for forever:**
- The above. Monthly subscription as long as you publish.

**What you give up:**
- The subscriber list lives on beehiiv. Your access depends on beehiiv's terms. The Buttondown incident this year (Evoked's account deleted without warning) is the cautionary tale.
- Editorial control over the email's HTML/CSS. You get what beehiiv's template engine produces.
- Future migration cost. If you ever leave beehiiv, exporting and re-importing the list is real work.

**Who this is right for:**
- Publishers who want to focus on writing, not infrastructure
- Lists above ~1,000 subscribers where deliverability matters at scale
- Publishers comfortable with the platform-tenancy trade-off

## Option B: Build custom on Resend + Neon

**Cost:**
- Resend: free tier sends 3,000 emails/month and 100/day. $20/month for 50,000 emails. We are very likely under the free tier for the foreseeable future.
- Neon Postgres: free tier (we are already using it).
- **Effective ongoing cost: $0/month** until the list grows past Resend's free tier.

**Build cost (one-time):**
- Engineering: 2-3 hours of focused work for the basic version. Components:
  - Add `newsletter_subscribers` table to Neon (schema, migrations)
  - Modify `/api/newsletter/subscribe` to store in Neon in addition to (or instead of) beehiiv
  - Build unsubscribe endpoint + token-based unsubscribe URL
  - Build send CLI: `npm run newsletter:send <markdown-file>` that reads a markdown file, renders to HTML, sends via Resend to all active subscribers
  - Build optional admin endpoint to list subscribers, mark unsubscribes, export CSV
- DNS: verify `evoked.dev` as a sending domain in Resend (similar process to beehiiv, but cleaner because we control both ends)

**What you get:**
- Subscribers in YOUR database. Exportable, owned, never platform-revokeable.
- Emails sent from `erin@evoked.dev`. Looks personal, not "powered by beehiiv".
- Newsletters as markdown in your repo. Version-controlled, peer-reviewable (via PR like everything else), recoverable.
- Brand consistency end to end. Subscribers see evoked.dev, evoked.dev content, evoked.dev sender. No beehiiv branding in the chain.
- Cost predictability. $0 until the list is large; even then, modest.
- One less account to manage.

**What you give up:**
- **Open rate / click-through analytics** unless you build pixel tracking and link-rewriting (controversial because Vuong/McEnerney-disciplined publications often choose NOT to track readers).
- **Subscriber growth helpers** (referral programs, recommendation engines, embeddable widgets) that beehiiv offers.
- **Deliverability tuning** that beehiiv does for you. Resend handles the basics but you are responsible for sending reputation. Start small, send well-written content, and reputation grows; do it poorly and you land in spam.
- **WYSIWYG editor.** You write markdown. For founder-direct sends this is arguably a feature, not a bug.

**Who this is right for:**
- Publishers committed to sovereignty as a brand principle (Evoked specifically)
- Smaller lists where deliverability tuning is not yet critical
- Publishers comfortable shipping their own infrastructure (you have shipped 50 PRs today; you are this person)
- Publishers who do not want to track readers and do not want analytics that work against the relationship

## Option C: Hybrid

Keep beehiiv as the primary sender, but ALSO store every subscriber in Neon Postgres.

**Build cost:** ~1 hour. Just modify `/api/newsletter/subscribe` to write to Neon in addition to posting to beehiiv. The send pipeline stays on beehiiv.

**What this gives you:**
- Sovereignty backup. If beehiiv ever fails (Buttondown-style), you have the list to migrate to your own custom system within a day.
- Optionality. You can build the custom send pipeline later when you have time, run it in parallel for comparison, then cut over when ready.
- Zero disruption to current working state.

**What it costs:**
- A small amount of code (subscriber sync logic). The duplication overhead is low because beehiiv is the canonical sender; Neon is just the safety copy.

**Who this is right for:**
- Anyone uncertain about the long-term platform call who wants to keep current operations stable while protecting the option to leave later.

This is the "have your cake and eat it" option. The cost is low. The value is real.

## What about sending from iCloud?

You asked about this specifically. Honest answer: **not for bulk newsletter sends.** Two reasons:

1. **Rate limits.** iCloud SMTP throttles aggressively for personal accounts (around 50-100 sends per day before they slow you down or block you). This is fine for sending one-off emails to mom or a friend, not for a growing newsletter list.
2. **Reputation.** iCloud's outgoing IP is shared with millions of personal accounts and is not optimized for newsletter delivery. Your messages would land in promotions tabs or spam folders disproportionately.

What "feels like iCloud" without actually using iCloud SMTP: send from `erin@evoked.dev` via Resend with sender authentication done right. Subscribers see a personal-looking sender from your own domain. Replies route to your actual inbox (iCloud or wherever you read mail). The effect is the same brand and personal feel; the infrastructure is built for the job.

If you genuinely want the literal `erin.stanley358@icloud.com` From line, the only path is sending small batches manually from Apple Mail or another email client. Some publishers do this for their first 50-100 subscribers as a "this is hand-crafted" signal. It does not scale, but it is authentic.

## Decision matrix

Five questions to sit with. The answers tip the scale.

| Question | Tips toward beehiiv | Tips toward custom |
|---|---|---|
| Do I expect to write more newsletters than I want to maintain infrastructure? | Yes → beehiiv | No → custom |
| Is brand sovereignty (no platform branding in subscriber inboxes) load-bearing for Evoked? | No → beehiiv is fine | Yes → custom |
| Do I want analytics on opens/clicks? | Yes → beehiiv | No, or I am philosophically against tracking → custom |
| Am I comfortable doing 2-3 hours of engineering on the send pipeline? | No → beehiiv | Yes → custom |
| Do I want a per-month platform fee in my cost structure forever? | Yes, in exchange for the polish → beehiiv | No → custom |

If three or more answers tip toward custom, custom is your answer.
